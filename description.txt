This project focuses on designing a DC lifting magnet using the Finite Element Method Magnetics (FEMM) software. The primary objective is to develop a lightweight and efficient lifting magnet that meets specified design requirements while minimizing losses. Through this assignment, students will gain practical experience with finite-element techniques and FEMM, exploring both its advantages and limitations. The design process involves creating an initial concept based on fundamental electromagnetics, followed by iterative optimization in FEMM to achieve the desired performance. Key deliverables include the magnet's estimated weight, losses, and the required coil voltage to hold a given mass at specified air gaps (1mm and 5mm). Additionally, the variation of coil inductance with air gap will be analyzed to inform system voltage requirements during operation. This hands-on exercise emphasizes creativity and individual problem-solving, reinforcing the understanding of electromagnetic design principles while preparing students for practical engineering challenges.
